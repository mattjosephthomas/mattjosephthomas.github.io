Making Innovation Real: Whereabouts and the 2024 Tourism Innovator of the Year

Matthew Hardy Thomas 2024

Tourism Technology Company, the company behind Whereabouts, was awarded the 2024 Tourism Innovator of the Year by the Tourism Industry Association of Ontario (TIAO). It’s a recognition that means a great deal to me, not just for what it says about our work, but for how unlikely the whole journey has been.

I’m one of four co-founders of TTC. Mike Jacobs, who runs an excellent tourism marketing company called The New Business, and two other partners. Together we’ve built Whereabouts into something we’re proud of: a platform that gives destination marketing organizations the CRM, data, booking, and itinerary tools they need to actually support their local tourism businesses, not just promote them from a distance.

Finding the Sleeping Giants

The idea for Whereabouts first took shape while I was doing my Master’s in Strategic Foresight and Innovation at OCAD University. I was studying the tourism system, this enormous interconnected web of businesses, governments, travellers, and communities, and I kept coming back to the same question: What are these DMOs? They seem like sleeping giants in the industry. What can I do to wake them up?

DMOs sit at the centre of it all. They run the campaigns that get people dreaming about a destination. But when a traveller is finally ready to book, the journey falls apart. They get redirected off the DMO site, they land on an OTA like Expedia, and up to 30% of the revenue leaves the community. The DMO did the work, but someone else cashed the cheque. I thought there had to be a better way. Thankfully, I found co-founders who agreed.

Innovation with Your Head in the Clouds

My whole time at OCAD was wonderful. Stimulating, expansive, full of big ideas about systems thinking and design and futures. But I could feel, the entire time, that my head was securely in the clouds. Maybe even a little further up than that. The programme was incredible, but “strategic foresight” doesn’t pay the bills unless you can make the innovation real.

One of the books that inspired me was Scott Adams’ How to Fail at Almost Everything and Still Win Big. The core idea stuck with me: you don’t need a single grand plan. You need a system, and you need to keep trying things. So I did. LondonFuse. Beach Bus. Parkbus. City Proper. Always connected to tourism in some way, and each one taught me something new about how the industry actually works. I like to think of myself as a designer of systems, and each of those ventures gave me a clearer picture of the system I wanted to design.

Finding My Sandbox

For my personality type, at least, the best thing I could do was find a sandbox with some nice tall walls that let me play inside it. Tourism turned out to be that sandbox. There’s real room for improvement in the industry, but it’s also wonderfully well-connected and full of great people.

With Whereabouts, I finally got the canvas. We built a CRM that gives destination managers real tools to collaborate with their businesses and stakeholders. We built booking technology that keeps revenue in the community instead of leaking it to OTAs. We built interactive itinerary tools that turn “just looking” into actual trips. In one pilot with Northeastern Ontario Tourism, a new accommodation business generated $10,000 in bookings in its first week through our platform. Our itinerary tool increased engagement by 78% compared to traditional pages.

What the Award Means

Getting recognized with this award is meaningful because it comes from the industry itself. These are the people I’ve worked alongside for over a decade. Tourism is a community before it’s an industry, and being recognized by your community is something that stays with you. It was nice to be there in person.

We’re just getting started. If you’re a destination manager curious about what Whereabouts can do, I’d love to hear from you.

Matthew Thomas is co-founder of Tourism Technology Company (Whereabouts) and CEO of Parkbus, Canada’s national outdoor experience company. He holds a Master’s in Strategic Foresight and Innovation from OCAD University and was named Innovator of the Year at the 2024 Ontario Tourism Summit.
